## Image-by-Image Details

### 1. Potter's Hands on Wheel (jingdezhen-alt-1.jpg)
- **Source:** Wikimedia Commons (CC BY-SA 4.0)
- **Photographer:** Unknown (Wikimedia contributor)
- **Description:** Close-up of hands shaping wet clay on a traditional manual potter's wheel. Rich terracotta and earthy brown tones, natural warm lighting. Documentary-style shot with motion blur on the spinning wheel.
- **Why chosen:** The warm caramel/terracotta palette anchors the gallery's earthy tone. The intimate hands-on-wheel composition is the quintessential ceramics workshop image. Excellent resolution (6000px wide) allows for generous cropping.

### 2. Traditional Kiln Site (jingdezhen-alt-2.jpg)
- **Source:** Wikimedia Commons (KOGL Type 1 / Korea Heritage Service)
- **Photographer:** Korea Heritage Service
- **Description:** The Goryeo Celadon Kiln Site in Gangjin, Korea. A traditional East Asian climbing/dragon kiln structure built into a hillside. Stone and earth construction with visible kiln chambers. Natural outdoor lighting on a historic archaeological site.
- **Why chosen:** While Korean rather than Chinese, this image represents the same East Asian dragon kiln tradition used in Jingdezhen. At 16:9 ratio, it provides visual variety in the gallery. The stone ruins against natural hillside create dramatic, ancient atmosphere. Directly relevant to the kiln fire search term.

### 3. Taoxichuan Architecture (jingdezhen-alt-3.jpg)
- **Source:** Wikimedia Commons (CC BY-SA 4.0)
- **Photographer:** Simon Wade
- **Description:** The Taoxichuan Cultural and Creative Block in Jingdezhen -- a former ceramic factory converted into a cultural district. Red brick industrial architecture with contemporary design elements, clear blue sky backdrop.
- **Why chosen:** The ONLY directly Jingdezhen-specific image in the set. Shows the real architectural character of the city's creative district. The red brick provides warm tones consistent with the gallery palette. This is the location the tour route is about.

### 4. Porcelain Painting Artisan (jingdezhen-alt-4.jpg)
- **Source:** Wikimedia Commons (CC BY-SA 4.0)
- **Photographer:** Davidi Vardi
- **Description:** Porcelain products after the decoration and painting stage at the Herend Porcelain Museum (Hungary). Shows fine brushwork detail on white porcelain surfaces with blue decorative patterns. Multiple pieces visible on workbenches.
- **Why chosen:** Best available image of porcelain painting in action. The blue-on-white color scheme provides the essential cobalt blue accent to the gallery palette. Professional museum/documentary quality, not a tourist snapshot. High resolution (5472px) allows cropping to 16:10.

### 5. Museum Display of Historical Porcelain (jingdezhen-alt-5.jpg)
- **Source:** Wikimedia Commons (Public Domain / WorldHistoryPics)
- **Photographer:** Gary Todd (WorldHistoryPics project)
- **Description:** Ming dynasty Jingdezhen porcelain from the Hongzhi reign period (1488-1505), photographed in a museum display setting. Blue underglaze decoration on white porcelain, classical Chinese motifs.
- **Why chosen:** Authentic Jingdezhen Ming dynasty piece -- directly relevant to the tour's historical context. The museum lighting provides clean, professional presentation. The blue-and-white color scheme ties into the gallery's cobalt blue accent while maintaining the cream/white base. Public Domain license allows unrestricted use.
